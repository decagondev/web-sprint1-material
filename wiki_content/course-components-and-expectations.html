Welcome to the Full Stack Web Development program at BloomTech! You may be thinking, "What am I doing here? What do Core Competencies mean? What is a Guided Project? What are Practice activities?"

At BloomTech, we may do things a little differently from how you may have experienced a classroom previously. As you continue the program, you will come to know and understand our terms more thoroughly. However, here are some quick requirements and definitions to get you started.

Course Components

Core Competencies

  • A compilation of reading, videos, and practice problems to help you learn how to code. This material is intended to help you become familiar and comfortable with each concept, and it prepares you to cover more in-depth learning experiences such as Guided Projects and Module Projects (see below).

Guided Project

  • A facilitated, interactive lesson led by one of BloomTech's instructors. The instructor will take the concepts you are learning and apply them in circumstances similar to what you'll see in the industry. 

Practice Activity: Module Projects

  • Module Projects are coding projects that you will do on your own. Like Guided Projects, Module Projects contain challenging problems similar to what you will see in the industry. You will assess the problems and apply your new knowledge to solve them. Each project will have a way to verify if your solutions are correct. Module Project problems are similar to what you'll see on the Sprint Challenge, making them the best tool to prepare for it.

Check for Understanding

  • A short formative* assessment aimed to see if you retain the information during the learning process. These quizzes can help you know of any areas you need to review. 

Sprint Challenge

  • Sprint Challenges are one of the two required assignments you have each Sprint (the Sprint Assessment is the other). Each Sprint Challenge includes a codebase similar to one a Full-Stack Web Software Engineer works in, where you will demonstrate mastery of your newfound skills from the Sprint. You will receive a set of problems that you will need to complete to move on to the next Sprint. 

Sprint Assessment

  • A summative** test with multiple-choice, multiple true-false, and free-response questions covering the learning objectives from a sprint. You must achieve at least an 80% on this assessment to move on to the next Sprint.

*Formative Assessment: An assessment given for the learner's benefit. This helps you figure out what you need to know and helps us figure out where the class is struggling to address it immediately.

**Summative Assessment: An assessment that evaluates your mastery of a subject. For example, the sprint assessment.

 

Engagement Expectations

Attendance and participation in BloomTech courses are measured in multiple ways, including but not limited to taking attendance in live instruction experiences, verifying module & sprint project attempts, and confirming activity in BloomTech’s Learning Management Systems.

While learners may choose to attend live or view recorded sessions, attendance and engagement with the material are critical to success in the program. Learners who do not have activity on the Learning Management Systems for more than seven (7) days will be flagged as inactive, and BloomTech will reach out to verify enrollment.

BloomTech may withdraw inactive learners to protect them from unintentional further vesting of tuition.

Learners who are unable to engage with the curriculum and make satisfactory progress due to extenuating circumstances may need to take a leave of absence.

 

How to Pass Your Sprints

How To Pass A Technical Sprint: 

  • Receive a 100% on the Sprint Challenge
    • A Sprint Challenge is a time-bound project that you must complete using the skills you’ve learned in each Sprint.
    • After you make your first attempt at a Sprint Challenge, you will have unlimited attempts to get a passing grade of 100%.
  • Receive 80% on the Sprint Assessment
    • A Sprint Assessment is a multiple-choice test that checks for understanding of the knowledge you’ve gained throughout the Sprint.
    • After you make your first attempt at a Sprint Assessment, you will have unlimited attempts to get a passing grade of 80%.

How To Pass an ACS Sprint:

You must complete the Sprint Challenge requirement:

  • Attempt the official General Code Assessment test (GCA)
  • Complete the corresponding career readiness assignment

Instructions are provided in the respective sprints on what you must submit for each sprint challenge.
